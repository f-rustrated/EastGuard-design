# B3: Replication Protocol

## 1. Fan-out: leader sends batch to all followers in parallel

**Why:** Chain replication reduces leader bandwidth but adds sequential network hops, increasing latency with each
additional replica. With EastGuard's replication factor ≤ 5, the bandwidth benefit of chain replication is negligible.
Fan-out is simpler and lower latency.

---

## 2. Majority quorum ACK before returning success to producer

Required follower fsyncs = `R - Q` (where R = replication factor, Q = majority quorum)

| R | Q | Follower fsyncs required |
|---|---|--------------------------|
| 3 | 2 | 1                        |
| 5 | 3 | 2                        |

**Why:** Leader is always fsynced. Total durable copies = leader + (R - Q) = Q. Even if the maximum number of nodes
fail, at least one durable copy survives. Waiting for all replicas (R) blocks on any single slow node; waiting for just
one sacrifices durability on leader failure.

---

## 3. Mid-batch crash recovery is handled by Raft

If followers crash mid-batch after the leader has ACK'd:

- Surviving replica with the most up-to-date log wins leader election (Raft guarantee)
- Recovered followers send their `last_applied_offset` to the new leader
- New leader streams missing entries; follower fsyncs and rejoins quorum

**Why no separate mechanism:** Raft's log replication and election protocol already guarantee that a node with a stale
log cannot be elected leader, and that lagging followers catch up via log streaming. EastGuard delegates this entirely
to the Raft layer.
